Paddington, the Student Loan Debt Management Tool

The final project of our class was the financial app, Paddington. This was an interesting in that it was primarily coding based. For Paddington, we decided to follow the LEAN Model of AGILE and work within certain constraints. This project was created with Nichelle, Gaby, Paulina, and Merritt.


Diving In

Because of the time constraints and usage of the LEAN Model, we jumped right into creating user personas and a journey map.

Both Audrey and Devan provided great insights and a compass for us to follow throughout the design. However, Devan's financial situation was what influenced the numbers part of the app the most.

Devan's Emotional Journey

After creating Audrey and Devan, we created the final part of our pre-design phase, the journey map. Here we follow them on their ups and downs in order to discover where Paddington can help them.

Wireframing

Due to the time constraints, we decided to jump right into low-res wireframes, rather than paper wireframes. We made this decision in order to be able to test them more quickly.

Testing and Iteration

With the low-res wireframes completed, we tested and recieved some great insights. After the first round of insights, we quickly iterated. By this time though, the style guide was completed, so we applied that to our 2nd round wireframes.

A/B Testing

Now that the wireframes and style guide were paired together, we had high-res comps to test. This was done by A/B testing 5 users, 3 men and 2 women.


Test Results

The A/B Testing produced some great feedback and the designs were well recieved.

    The biggest takeaways from the testing were:

  • The Pig Logo was Not Intuitive
    The pig logo did not read as a homepage for many. Most users thought it was a
    piggy bank and thus for money.

  • Users Did Not Want to Put in Their Age
    Most users did not like having to put their age in, and did not see what the purpose was.
    They wanted to only stick to the relevant information regarding the loans

  • Sleek Sliders
    Most users preferred the thinner sliders and liked seeing the numbers change on the side


Coding the High-Res Comps

With the testing completed, we quickly finished our high res comps and got to coding. The following link will take you to the live version of the Paddington App.

Reflections for the Next Sprint

The Paddington App was my favorite project of the semester, due to it being so code heavy. I am very pleased with the end result and using the LEAN Method was very interesting because we had to prioritize what research to do and what to create.

    If we had time for another sprint we would have changed the following:

  • Different Coding Languages
    Currently, the app is coded with HTML, CSS, and javaScript.
    However, this makes it harder to test when on a desktop, so I would
    have liked to have created it with AngularJS, or a similar framework.

  • More Accurate Coding
    Because some of the designs were more difficult to code than others,
    we had to change certain elements in the final version. With more time,
    I would have liked to have coded the app to look 100% like the final comps.

  • To Have Been There From the Beginning
    Due to an unforseeable event, I was moved to this group halfway through.
    I would have liked to have done more in the research and testing phases.
