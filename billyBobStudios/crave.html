Crave, the Food Recommendation App

Crave is the food app centered around recommendations created from your eating habits and those of your friends and family. And was created in conjunction with Misha and Danny.


Discovering the Problem

Before we could even form a hypothesis, we had to find out people's habits. This was done by interviewing 3 people. and sending out surveys. (36 results)

I've got a problem...statement

After analyzing the research and looking at the trends, we arrived at the following conclusion.

Trent Moore

Once we had a problem statement, our next step was to create a persona, this would anchor us and ensure that we always designed with the end-user in mind.

What is Trent Thinking?

After creating Trent, we wanted to empathize with him even further. To get into his mind, his thoughts, and his shoes. This empathy map ties in closely with our Journey Map a little later.

What is Trent Feeling and Doing?

The next step in the process was visualizng Trent's feelings and his struggles.

Trent's Journey

Combining the storyboard and empathy map, we created the Journey Map, tapping even further into the emotions Trent feels along the way.

It's Design Time

With a clear vision of Trent in our mind and a solid foundation of research, we finally began the design phase of the project, starting with the User Flow.

First round of Wireframes

With the user flow charted out, we now had a clear plan of action and created what we thought were the perfect amount of wireframes...

Testing our designs and our assumptions

Armed with these new wireframes, we moved to one of the most crucial phases, testing. Because of Trent's age, career, and location, we decided to find people that most fit his description at the local coffee shop. We were able to test on 5 people, 3 females and 2 males.

    From this round of testing we discovered 3 key insights:

  • The Flow was Intuitive
    All of the testers found the flow easy to use and didn't have struggle at all.

  • They Enjoyed the Features
    All of the testers liked the wait time feature and the friend's recommendations section

  • We Needed Filters
    Most of the testers requested this feature for accessability reasons as well as to help
    them pick a food based on thier mood.

After this round of testing, we utilized the feedback and iterated on our wiresframes.

From the initial 4 wiresframes, we ended up adding 3 more for a total of 7. The additional three frames were to showcase the new filter options.

The final piece of the puzzle for this 2 week sprint

Now that we had researched, designed, tested, and iterated, we had finally reached the high res comps phase of the project.

Reflections and What We'd Do Next

Having completed the 3 high res comps for our user flows, we reached the end of our two week sprint. Based on our testing and feedback, we believe that our project was a success and would solve Trent's problem if we were to make this app.

    If we had time for another sprint we would have changed the following:

  • More Rounds of Testing
    Our testing was done in the wireframes stage and going forward we would test
    our high res comps to get even more feedback and improve the flows.

  • Adding a Second Persona
    Our designs, journey, flow, and storyboard were all created based around Trent Moore.
    However, if we had more time I would have liked to have added another persona,
    perhaps a female with different needs and goals from the app.

This would conclude our first project in class and our first experience working in the AGILE model
as well as using the double diamond to inform our decisions.
